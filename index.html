Dilettante

CLICK HERE TO VIEW

Aim

Dilettante is traditionally a pejorative term used to describe people who acquire a shallow but broad knowledgebase, as opposed to someone who might prefer a deeper but more narrow range of same. In contemporary society reading books, the best way of improving one's knowledge in a specific subject, is becoming increasingly rare. Instead, we consume a neverending supply of tweets, gossip and soundbites. Satirical television programs like Last Week Tonight are intended to push society towards an interest in long-term governance and policy issues, as opposed to political stories of the day, which are often trivial. Dilettente follows John Oliver's example, but for the subject areas in which the client has some expertise. It cannot be denied, however, that he is a product of the millenial generation. He embraces this simple fact by taking Dilettante as the name of his blog, then uses it to find the golden mean between broad and deep thinking.

Objectives

  • Showcase a selection of blog articles about a range of topics to the end-user while maintaining a fashionable, minimalist aesthetic.

  • Impose a structure on the client's personal learning and reading habits such that this sustained, consistent practice benefits his career while improving the quality of his ideas.

Features

  • A sleek, attractive, animated definition of the word 'dilettante' appears on home page on load (Javascript). This is a homage to Pulp Fiction.

  • An interactive triangular button removes this definition and loads the selection of articles (jQuery, Bootstrap)

  • The articles are displayed in a bootstrap grid for grid size optimization across screen sizes

  • The subjects the author writes about (Film, STEM, History) demonstrate to the user a little about his personal and professional interests (articles not yet written, he is focusing on his coding!)

  • An interactive 'navbar' that actually functions as a jQuery-based sorting mechanism (Unfinished)
  • Optimized across XL,L,M,S screen sizes (bootstrap standard). Mobile responsiveness not yet implemented.

WKL

CLICK HERE TO VIEW

Aim

The client, Wendy Knox-Leet, is a retired visual and performance artist who had developed an extensive portfolio of poems without any way to share them with the world. The purpose of this website is to create an environment for distributing her content and establishing her presence within London's poetry community.

Objectives

  • Showcase a selection of poems by the client while maintaining a sleek, abstract, bright, geometric aesthetic with an emphasis on verticality.

  • The subject of the matter is dark, but the client's personality in real life is gregarious, bright, charismatic, loving and outgoing. The design reflects this. Hopefully will encourage the client to create poetry that is more positive, and improve her overall outlook on life.

  • Develop the client's interest in poetry from a personal to a professional standard.

  • Establish the client's presence in the industry, such that she can easily get access to spoken word poetry events as a performer.

  • House a selection of media about the client.

Features

  • A dynamic, collapsible bootstrap navbar.

  • A hybrid CSS-Javascript animation on the home page with a slogan that summarizes the client and her poetry, created using the client's own poetry style as a precedent.

  • Poems categorized into 5 sections corresponding to provocative subject areas. Each section is an opportunity to capture the attention of the end user. Section design is intended to convey a sense of verticality to encourage passage throughout the website.

  • >40 poem pages, with a minimalistic design intended to encourage consumption and sharing of the material.

  • The last section of the website houses a selection of media relating to the client, which may surprise and engage users given the provocative nature of the subject matter above it.

  • Optimized across XL,L,M,S,XS screen sizes (bootstrap standard).

Budget Advisor

CLICK HERE TO VIEW

Aim

Websites like MoneyAdviceService operate on a simple principle. The user voluntarily provides data regarding their personal finances, their input is compared to databases of average values, then the website informs the user when their input deviates from it by a significant margin. The most sophisticated services in this sector also promote the uptake of relevant benefits, tax credits, etc. The simplicity of this model means that the barrier to entry for new competitors is quite low. Budget Advisor is a quick and easy effort to implement the basic functionality necessary for such an enterprise.

Objectives

  • Create a quick, simple input method for the user's financial data

  • Provide a basic analysis of the user's data which is broadly understandable

  • Inform the user how their input compares to national averages

Features

  • An ASP.Net input form linked to a Visual Studio MVC web application

  • Charts.js representation of the input data

  • Controller based data analysis

  • jQuery toggles for the input and output (Unfinished)
